Seizures

Epilepsy, First Fit, Provoked

First Unprovoked: Stop driving 12 months.

Provoked: Stop driving 6 months (if provoked by acute illness/meds).

Epilepsy: Must be seizure-free for 1 year to drive.

First Unprovoked: Stop driving 5 years (unless specific criteria met).

Epilepsy: Must be seizure-free for 10 years without medication.

Blackout / Syncope

TLoC, Vasovagal, Cough Syncope

Simple Faint (Vasovagal): Allowed if distinct prodrome & trigger. No driving restriction.

Unexplained: Stop driving 6 months.

Cough Syncope: Stop driving 6 months.

Simple Faint: Allowed if distinct prodrome.

Unexplained: Stop driving 12 months.

Cough Syncope: Stop driving 12 months.

Stroke / TIA

CVA, Mini-stroke

TIA/Stroke: Stop driving 1 month. No need to notify DVLA unless residual deficit after 1 month.

TIA/Stroke: Stop driving 1 year. Must notify DVLA. Requires functional assessment.

ACS (MI / Angina)

Heart Attack, Stent, Angioplasty

Angioplasty (Stent): Stop driving 1 week.

No Angioplasty: Stop driving 4 weeks.

Notify DVLA only if angina persists.

Arrhythmia / Pacemaker

AF, SVT, ICD

Arrhythmia: Stop 4 weeks if incapacitated.

Pacemaker: Stop 1 week.

ICD (Defib): Stop 6 months (Shock/Implant).

Arrhythmia: Stop until controlled (min 4 weeks).

Pacemaker: Stop 6 weeks.

ICD: Permanent Bar from Group 2.

Diabetes

Insulin, Sulfonylureas, Hypo

Insulin: Notify DVLA. Check BM q2h while driving.

Severe Hypo: Stop driving if >1 episode in 12 months.

Insulin: Strict criteria. Must provide 6 weeks of glucose logs. Must notify DVLA.
