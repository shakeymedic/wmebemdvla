Seizures / Epilepsy

First Fit, Provoked, Epilepsy
  • First Unprovoked: Stop driving 6 months (if normal EEG/Scan) or 12 months (standard).
  • Epilepsy (2+ seizures): Stop driving 12 months seizure-free.
  • Provoked: Stop driving 6 months (e.g., related to acute illness).
  • Sleep Only: Allowed if pattern established for 1 year.
  • First Unprovoked: Stop driving 5 years. (Must be seizure-free and off meds).
  • Epilepsy: Stop driving 10 years. (Must be seizure-free and off meds for 10 years).
  • Provoked: DVLA assessment required. Likely 6 months minimum.

Blackout / Syncope (TLoC)

Vasovagal, Unexplained, Cough Syncope
  • Simple Faint (Vasovagal): Allowed if distinct prodrome (warning) & known trigger. No restriction ("3 P's" - Posture, Provocation, Prodrome).
  • Unexplained (Low Risk): Stop driving 6 months.
  • Unexplained (High Risk): Stop driving 6 months (or 4 weeks if cause treated).
  • Cough Syncope: Stop driving 6 months.
  • Simple Faint: Allowed if distinct prodrome.
  • Unexplained: Stop driving 12 months (strict).
  • Cough Syncope: Stop driving 12 months.

Stroke & TIA

CVA, Mini-stroke, Amaurosis Fugax
  • Single TIA: Stop driving 1 month. DO NOT notify DVLA.
  • Multiple TIAs: Stop driving 1 month. Notify DVLA if recurrent.
  • Stroke: Stop driving 1 month. Notify DVLA only if residual deficit (visual/cognitive) persists >1 month.
  • All Stroke/TIA: Stop driving 1 year.
  • Notification: You MUST notify DVLA.
  • Re-licensing: Requires functional assessment and no significant deficit.

ACS / Angina / Angioplasty

MI, NSTEMI, Stent
  • Angioplasty (Stent): Stop driving 1 week (if successful).
  • No Angioplasty: Stop driving 4 weeks.
  • Angina: Must not drive if symptoms occur at rest or while driving.
  • Notify DVLA: Only if symptoms persist or restart.
  • All Events: Stop driving 6 weeks minimum.
  • Requirement: Functional test (LVEF ‚â•40%) required before restart.
  • Notification: MUST notify DVLA.

Arrhythmia / Devices

AF, Pacemaker, ICD
  • Pacemaker: Stop driving 1 week post-implant.
  • ICD (Defib): Stop 6 months (if for shock/therapy). Stop 1 month (if prophylactic).
  • Arrhythmia: Stop 4 weeks if it caused incapacity/syncope.
  • Pacemaker: Stop driving 6 weeks.
  • ICD (Defib): PERMANENT BAR (Licence Refused/Revoked).
  • Arrhythmia: Stop until controlled (min 4 weeks) & LVEF ‚â•40%.

Diabetes

Insulin, Sulfonylureas, Hypo
  • Insulin: Notify DVLA. Must test blood sugar q2h while driving.
  • Tablets: No restriction usually (unless prone to severe hypos).
  • Severe Hypo: Stop driving if >1 episode in 12 months (awake).
  • Insulin: Notify DVLA. Strict annual review. Must use finger-prick glucose (not just Flash/CGM) for driving logs.
  • Hypoglycaemia: Full awareness required. No severe hypos in 12 months.

Alcohol & Drugs

Dependence vs Misuse
  • Persistent Misuse: Stop driving 6 months min.
  • Dependence: Stop driving 1 year min.
  • Persistent Misuse: Stop driving 1 year min.
  • Dependence: Stop driving 3 years min.

"Misuse" = adverse consequences. "Dependence" = addiction/withdrawal.
